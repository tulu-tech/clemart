The Sacred Geometry

The Art of Tezhip

Illuminating the Divine Word with 24K Gold

Explore the Collection
ClemArt — Classical Arts
Scroll

"Emerging from the infinitesimal point of creation, dissolving into nothingness..."

Centered around the illumination of holy manuscripts, Tezhip is the sacred discipline of gold ornamentation. Its roots run deep into the past, its trunk stands firm in the present, and its fruits stretch into posterity.

Today, as in centuries past, Tezhip remains the undisputed jewel of classical arts. It is the most delicate and naive garment of the Divine Word, opening new horizons through the hands of master illuminators.

The Masterworks Exhibition

Sultan Ahmet I. Tuğra

An imposing masterpiece set on a dark canvas, utilizing solid 22-karat gold inlay and acrylics. The calligraphy draws the eye inward, embodying the authority and spiritual weight of the Ottoman tuğra.

Material 22K Gold & Acrylic
Canvas Black Vellum Design

Sultan Selim III. Epoch

Executed with the classic 'Zerendut' technique, meaning 'brushed in gold'. Every stroke of the master's brush applies a mixture of crushed 22-karat gold leaf and specialized gouache over a meticulously primed surface.

Technique Zerendut Hat
Material 22K Gold & Gouache
